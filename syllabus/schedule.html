Tentative Course Schedule

Week Date Title Topics Covered Reading Assignments Assignments
1 Tue, Jan 21 - Thu, Jan 23 Introduction to Experimental Design Purpose of experimentation, observational studies vs. experiments, key principles (randomization, replication, control). Dean et al., Ch. 1 ; Ch. 2 (Sec. 2.1–2.2) Assignment 1: Basics of Experimental Design (due Week 2)
2 Tue, Jan 28 - Thu, Jan 30 Completely Randomized Designs (CRD) Randomization procedures, CRD structure, one-way ANOVA model, assumptions, conducting ANOVA in R. Dean et al., Ch. 3 (Sec. 3.1–3.3, 3.4–3.5) Assignment 2: CRD Analysis (due Week 3)
3 Tue, Feb 4 - Thu, Feb 6 Blocking and RCBD Concept of blocking, RCBD setup, statistical models, ANOVA for RCBD, assessing blocking efficiency. Dean et al., Ch. 10 (Sec. 10.1–10.4) Assignment 3: RCBD Analysis (due Week 4)
4 Tue, Feb 11 - Thu, Feb 13 Multiple Comparisons & Contrasts Post-hoc tests (Tukey, Bonferroni, Scheffé), linear contrasts, orthogonal vs. non-orthogonal comparisons. Dean et al., Ch. 4 (Sec. 4.1–4.4) Assignment 4: Multiple Comparisons (due Week 5)
5 Tue, Feb 18 - Thu, Feb 20 Checking Model Assumptions Residual analysis, detecting non-normality, transformations, addressing heteroscedasticity, outliers. Dean et al., Ch. 5 (Sec. 5.1–5.3) Assignment 5: Residual Analysis (due Week 6)
6 Tue, Feb 25 - Thu, Feb 27 Factorial Designs Two-factor factorial designs, main effects, interactions, graphical representation, two-way ANOVA. Dean et al., Ch. 6 (Sec. 6.1–6.5) Assignment 6: Two-Factor ANOVA (due Week 7)
7 Tue, Mar 4 - Thu, Mar 6 Midterm Exam & Review Review of foundational concepts from Weeks 1–6; midterm exam covering CRD, RCBD, contrasts, factorial designs. Review all previous chapters and notes. Midterm Exam (Week 7)
8 Tue, Mar 11 - Thu, Mar 13 Higher-Order Factorial Designs Three-way factorial designs, higher-order interactions, dealing with unbalanced designs. Dean et al., Ch. 7 (Sec. 7.1–7.6) Assignment 7: Three-Way Factorial Analysis (due Week 9)
Tue, Mar 18 - Thu, Mar 20 Spring Break No class
9 Tue, Mar 25 - Thu, Mar 27 Analysis of Covariance (ANCOVA) Adjusting for covariates, reducing error variance, hypothesis testing, adjusting treatment means. Dean et al., Ch. 9 (Sec. 9.1–9.4) Assignment 8: ANCOVA Analysis (due Week 10)
10 Tue, Apr 1 - Thu, Apr 3 Complete Block Designs Extension of RCBD, randomization procedures, factorial treatments in blocks, variance components analysis. Dean et al., Ch. 10 (Sec. 10.6–10.8) Assignment 9: Complete Block Designs (due Week 11)
11 Tue, Apr 8 - Thu, Apr 10 Row–Column Designs Designs with two blocking factors (Latin squares, Youden designs), randomization procedures, ANOVA. Dean et al., Ch. 12 (Sec. 12.1–12.4) Assignment 10: Row–Column Analysis (due Week 12)
12 Tue, Apr 15 - Thu, Apr 17 Random-Effects & Mixed-Effects Models Fixed vs. random effects, single random-effect models, variance component estimation, REML estimation. Dean et al., Ch. 17 (Sec. 17.1–17.8) Assignment 11: Random and Mixed Models (due Week 13)
13 Tue, Apr 22 - Thu, Apr 24 Nested Models Nested models, random-effects nested models, variance components for nested factors, analysis in R. Dean et al., Ch. 18 (Sec. 18.1–18.4) Assignment 12: Nested Models (due Week 14)
14 Tue, Apr 29 - Thu, May 1 Split-Plot Designs Split-plot designs, whole plots and split plots, statistical models, ANOVA, split-split-plot designs. Dean et al., Ch. 19 (Sec. 19.1–19.5) Assignment 13: Split-Plot Analysis (due Week 15)
15 Tue, May 6 - Thu, May 8 Catching Up & Adjustments Reviewing and covering additional materials as needed.
16 Tue, May 13 - Thu, May 15 Review & Final Exam Comprehensive review and final exam preparation; administering final exam. Review all chapters and notes. Final Exam (Week 15)
